Interdisciplinary Projects and Research

1. Dynamic Braille Display for Accessible Digital Reading

Disciplines: Embedded Systems, Human–Computer Interaction, Assistive Technology

Overview: Developed a dynamic Braille display prototype aimed at enabling visually impaired users to read digital content in real time. The system converts on-screen text into tactile Braille patterns using an array of actuated pins, allowing seamless interaction with e-books, web pages, and educational materials.

Methodology: Utilized a microcontroller-driven piezoelectric/solenoid pin-matrix mechanism to form programmable Braille cells. Implemented a text-processing pipeline using NLP-based Braille translation algorithms supporting Grade-1 and Grade-2 Braille. Designed a low-latency driver interface for pin actuation, coupled with a power-efficient refresh strategy. Conducted usability testing to evaluate accuracy, tactile clarity, and reading comfort.

Outcomes: Achieved an average refresh latency of 120 ms per character and 94% translation accuracy across mixed-language datasets. The prototype demonstrated strong potential for affordable, portable assistive reading solutions. Results were showcased through hardware demonstrations and mock publication visuals.

3. Intelligent Quiz Web Application for OS, DBMS, and DAA

Global Awareness and Ethical Considerations

Case Study: The Global Impact of Algorithmic Bias in Healthcare

My work on the Neuro-Linguistic Interface was deeply influenced by the ethical dilemma of algorithmic bias. If the training data (EEG patterns) is primarily sourced from Western populations, the resulting model may perform poorly, or even dangerously, on individuals from globally diverse genetic or cultural backgrounds.

Analysis of Ethical Implications: This is a matter of Global Justice and Responsible AI. I implemented a rigorous data auditing pipeline to track demographic metadata and ensure proportional representation, or where not possible, clearly document the model's limitations regarding certain populations. This moves beyond local testing to a global, responsible deployment mindset.

Personal Reflection: I believe that as technologists, our responsibility extends far beyond writing functional code. Every line of code carries an implicit social contract, and demonstrating impact requires preemptive ethical reasoning to prevent harm across diverse global communities.
